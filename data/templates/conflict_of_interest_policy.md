# CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY
## {{entity_name}}
### A {{state_name}} Nonprofit Corporation

**Adopted:** {{effective_date}}

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to protect {{entity_name}} when it is considering a transaction or arrangement that might benefit the private interest of a director, officer, or key person.

## 2. Duty to Disclose

Any director, officer, or key person who has an actual or potential conflict of interest shall disclose the existence and nature of the conflict to the Board of Directors before the Corporation takes action on the matter.

## 3. Determining Whether a Conflict Exists

After disclosure, the interested person shall leave the meeting while the Board discusses and votes on whether a conflict exists.

## 4. Procedures for Addressing a Conflict

If the Board determines that a conflict exists, the Board shall consider alternatives and may approve the transaction only if it determines that the transaction is fair, reasonable, and in the Corporation's best interests.

The interested person may not vote on the transaction.

## 5. Records

Meeting minutes shall record the disclosure, the Board's determination, the names of persons present, the alternatives considered, and the vote.

## 6. Annual Statements

Each director and officer shall annually sign a statement affirming that they received, read, understand, and agree to comply with this policy.
