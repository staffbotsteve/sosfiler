# Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs)

## 1. The First-Time Entrepreneur (The Skeptic)
- **Demographics:** 25-40 years old, starting a side hustle, freelance business, or small local shop. Moderate income, highly sensitive to unexpected costs.
- **Pain Points:** Overwhelmed by legal jargon. Doesn't know the difference between an EIN and Articles of Organization. Terrified of doing it wrong and getting fined by the IRS. 
- **Objections:** "Is it really $49? Everyone else says $0 but then charges me $300."
- **Where they hang out:** Reddit (/r/Entrepreneur, /r/smallbusiness), YouTube tutorials, TikTok business channels.
- **What messaging resonates:** Transparency. Breaking down exactly what they need to open a bank account (Formation + EIN + Operating Agreement) and showing how competitors charge $250+ for it.

## 2. The Existing Business Owner (The Expander)
- **Demographics:** 35-55 years old, already runs a successful business. Needs to form a new entity for a real estate purchase, a new brand (DBA), or a joint venture.
- **Pain Points:** Time is money. They know how the process works but hate doing the manual paperwork on clunky state websites. They despise LegalZoom's upsell flow because they know exactly what they *don't* need.
- **Objections:** "Is your software actually faster than me just doing it on the Secretary of State website?"
- **Where they hang out:** LinkedIn, industry-specific forums, real estate networking groups.
- **What messaging resonates:** Speed and utility. "No upsells to click through. Just give us the details and we'll file it today."

## 3. The Serial Entrepreneur (The Bulk Filer)
- **Demographics:** 30-50 years old. Starts multiple e-commerce brands, software products, or holds multiple properties. 
- **Pain Points:** Sick of paying $200+ per entity just to get an EIN and standard docs. Hates managing multiple auto-renewing registered agent subscriptions across different services.
- **Objections:** "Can I manage all my entities in one place without getting spammed?"
- **Where they hang out:** Twitter/X (Tech/VC circles), IndieHackers, specialized Slack/Discord communities.
- **What messaging resonates:** The flat, predictable cost. "Stop paying the LegalZoom tax on every new idea."
