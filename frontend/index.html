Questions, honestly answered

Frequently asked questions.

Is $29 really the SOSFiler service fee? What's the catch?
No catch. $29 is the full SOSFiler service fee, plus the official state filing fee, which goes straight to the government and is never marked up. Our software maps your details directly to state forms, prepares the package, and routes it through automation with operator backup only where the portal requires it. Lower overhead is what lets us charge what we charge.
What are the ongoing costs?
Annual report filing service is $29 plus official state fees. Registered agent service is optional and partner-assisted. No auto-renewals — we email you when a renewal or annual filing is coming due and you choose whether to continue.
How fast is the filing?
We prepare your filing immediately and submit through the fastest verified channel available for your state. Some portals support smooth automation; others require operator verification, CAPTCHA, or account steps. Your dashboard shows evidence-backed status updates — no guesswork.
Do I need a lawyer?
For a standard LLC formation, typically no. Our documents are based on state-specific legal templates and customized with your information. If you have complex ownership structures, IP concerns, or unusual arrangements, we recommend an attorney.
What state should I form my LLC in?
Usually your home state — where you live and do business. While Delaware, Wyoming, and Nevada are popular for specific situations, forming in a different state means you'll also need to register as a "foreign LLC" in your home state (extra cost and paperwork). Our wizard helps you decide.
What's a registered agent?
Every LLC must have a registered agent — a person or service authorized to receive legal documents and government notices on behalf of your business, with a physical address in your state of formation. You can use your own or choose partner-assisted registered agent coverage where available.
Can I cancel or get a refund?
If we haven't filed your documents yet, full refund. After filing, we can't refund a state fee already paid to the state, but we refund the SOSFiler service fee under our posted service guarantee.
How does the privacy organizer thing work?
In most states, the Organizer who signs the formation document goes on the public record. SOSFiler can sign as Organizer for you so your personal name and address stay off the public filing. Immediately after formation we transfer all Organizer authority back to you via a signed Statement of Organizer.
