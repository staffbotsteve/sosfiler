# SOSFiler World-Class Redesign Direction

Date: 2026-05-11

## Executive Direction

SOSFiler should feel like the fastest accountable way to form and manage an entity, not like another legal-template checkout funnel.

The core promise:

- Formation: $29 plus official state fees.
- Annual filings: $29 plus official state fees.
- No SOSFiler expedite fee.
- Fastest compliant filing lane by default.
- AI-first preparation, validation, filing execution, evidence capture, and dashboard updates.
- Operator touch only when an official portal requires login, CAPTCHA, identity, payment, terms, attestation, partner approval, or a protected action.

Do not claim unsafe automation. Do not imply SOSFiler bypasses state controls. The speed claim should be framed as:

> SOSFiler routes every filing through the fastest compliant lane available. If we miss a posted SOSFiler-controlled fulfillment target, the SOSFiler service fee is refundable under the posted service guarantee.

Avoid an absolute "faster than every competitor in every circumstance" claim until benchmark evidence exists across states and filing types.

- CorpNet annual report service: https://www.corpnet.com/annual-report-service/
- Harbor Compliance annual report service: https://www.harborcompliance.com/annual-report-service

### What Works

Tailor Brands has the easiest top-of-funnel: it asks for a small amount of context and makes the user feel guided. The weakness is that the broader brand bundle can feel less filing-specific.

Bizee has strong price discovery: entity type plus state leads quickly into package comparison. The weakness is package/add-on complexity.

Northwest feels trustworthy because it is plain-spoken, privacy-oriented, and less visually noisy. The weakness is that the path can feel content-heavy rather than product-led.

ZenBusiness feels polished and guided, with a modern dashboard orientation. The weakness is plan and renewal complexity.

LegalZoom has brand trust and clear package hierarchy. The weakness is that it feels like a legal-products marketplace with upsells rather than a focused automation platform.

Rocket Lawyer is strongest for legal membership framing. The weakness for SOSFiler's market is that subscriptions make the customer compare memberships rather than filing outcomes.

CorpNet and Harbor Compliance are useful references for annual report/compliance workflows. They feel B2B-service oriented, but their pricing and fulfillment are not as transparent or customer-dashboard-led as SOSFiler can be.

### What SOSFiler Should Borrow

- Tailor Brands: a low-friction guided start.
- Bizee: instant state/entity price visibility.
- Northwest: plain trust language and privacy seriousness.
- ZenBusiness: dashboard-forward lifecycle management.
- LegalZoom: confidence-building comparison tables.
- Harbor/CorpNet: compliance calendar and annual report seriousness.

### What SOSFiler Should Avoid

- Decorative icons and emoji.
- "Free" pricing that later requires add-ons to be useful.
- Auto-renewal ambiguity.
- Paid tiers that hide basic filing needs.
- Generic SaaS hero copy.
- Claiming full automation where official portals require a protected checkpoint.

## No-Icon Interface Rules

Use no decorative icons, emoji, mascot graphics, or icon-only controls.

Use these substitutes:

- Step numbers instead of step icons.
- Text status labels instead of checkmarks.
- Table cells that say "Included", "Add-on", "Plan-based", "Evidence-backed", or "Not offered".
- Plain progress bars and timelines.
- Strong typography, spacing, borders, and state labels.
- Short action buttons with verbs: "Start Filing", "Review Order", "Authorize Payment", "Open Dashboard".

OAuth buttons may keep required third-party brand marks only if paired with text.

## Formation Wizard Design

The wizard must feel shorter without collecting less data.

Use a two-column desktop layout:

- Left: current step form.
- Right: persistent order summary.

Step sequence:

1. Entity and state
   - Entity type
   - Formation state
   - Home state
   - Fulfillment lane and price shown immediately

2. Business name
   - Name entry
   - State naming hint
   - Name availability check if available

3. Business facts
   - Purpose
   - Principal address
   - Management type

4. Registered agent
   - Own agent
   - Partner-assisted agent, clearly optional and operator/partner-confirmed while CorpNet is pending

5. Owners and responsible party
   - Members/managers
   - EIN responsible party data
   - SSN/ITIN trust copy

6. Review and authorization
   - Official state fee
   - SOSFiler $29 fee
   - Optional partner/portal fees
   - Total authorization amount
   - Organizer/filing authorization checkboxes

7. Payment authorization
   - Manual-capture explanation
   - "We authorize estimated fees now and capture only after reconciliation."

Microcopy:

- "State fee" not "government fee" in most customer-facing areas.
- "SOSFiler service fee" not "platform fee".
- "Operator-assisted" should read as a professional fulfillment lane, not a fallback.
- "Protected checkpoint" should be used internally; public copy should say "official portal checkpoint".

## Customer Dashboard Design

The dashboard should answer five questions at all times:

1. What is the status?
2. What is SOSFiler doing now?
3. What, if anything, do I need to do?
4. What evidence/documents are available?
5. What will be due next?

Primary modules:

- Filing status timeline with text labels.
- Next action panel.
- Payment summary.
- Document vault.
- Annual filing and compliance calendar.
- Lifecycle actions: annual report, amendment, registered-agent change, certificate of status, certified copy, dissolution, withdrawal, reinstatement.

Remove emoji headings such as "Business Info" or "What To Do Next". Use plain headings.

## Operator Cockpit Design

Operator cockpit must be dense, text-first, and packet-led.

Primary order of information:

1. Required next action.
2. Safe-stop conditions.
3. Customer filing data.
4. Official portal route.
5. State-specific portal steps.
6. Payment/reconciliation requirements.
7. Required evidence.
8. Status action buttons.
9. Audit/event history.

The operator should rarely need to think, search, or reconstruct context. Every button should have a nearby evidence requirement.
